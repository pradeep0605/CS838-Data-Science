The idea behind The Map and the Territory itself is perhaps not a bad one \u2013 attempting to present a picture, an analysis of current trends, of things here and now and in flux, is challenging, but at the same time, it is, I think, necessary and appreciated by those who nevertheless would like to make sense of the world around them, to hear the opinions of those who are an active part of the current (cultural) landscape and who can offer an insightful analysis, venturing to do it without the benefit of hindsight. When done well, such works can be really thought-provoking and can have a long staying power that enhances the reader\u2019s being in the world and adds to the reader\u2019s critical engagement with it. The problem of The Map and the Territory in being far from this kind of book lies largely, I think, with its execution. To start with, the plot of the book is, how to say, nothing to write home about.
