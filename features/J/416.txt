Hole is engaged to investigate a bank robbery that went wrong, resulting in the death of a bank teller. A wider network of evil is uncovered and Hole is heavily tasked when he is implicated in one of the murders that follows. Nesbo\u2019s writing is the Good in this series. Like noir writers of the past, Nesbo can blend crime elements with a dark, somber setting and hold it all together with introspective and observant contemplations on a variety of subjects. Hole, through Nesbo, is an astute observer of human nature, society and the meaning of life. In Hole, Nesbo has created one of Nordic Noir\u2019s most endearing and charismatic players. Harry is assigned to Anna's case and - though it's being called a suicide - the detective is sure she was murdered. Harry's in a tricky position though: he has to investigate the killing without admitting he knew Anna....or he risks becoming a suspect himself. The book has a complex plot involving foreign travel, bank robbers, gypsies, unfaithful spouses, drug addicts, prison inmates, a rich executive, corrupt cops, and more.
